## high-2015-dissertation-cognitive-instructions-analytical-intuitive.pdf

**Author:** Dr. Donald R. High
**Institution:** Northcentral University, School of Psychology
**Year:** 2015
**Degree:** Doctor of Philosophy

**Title:** Effects of Cognitive Instructions for Discerning Analytical and Intuitive Tasks

**Relevance to Bot Watching:** This dissertation is the psychological foundation beneath the governance architecture. Dr. High's research on Dual System Theory (Kahneman, 2011) and cognitive instructions at decision points directly informs how the Governance Engine must present escalated decisions to human overseers.

Core finding: explicit instructions that help decision-makers recognize what kind of thinking a task requires — analytical or intuitive — significantly improve decision accuracy. This is the human-side complement to the AI-side Observability Layer. Together they address the WYSIATI problem: ensuring the human at the governance decision point has both complete information AND the right cognitive frame to process it.

The connection: governance escalation pathways must not just surface decisions — they must surface the TYPE of decision and prompt the correct thinking system before the human acts.
