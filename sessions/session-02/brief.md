Session 2 Topic

"What should intelligence — human or artificial — actually be optimizing for?"

This question was synthesized from the four topics each participant proposed at the close of Session 1. It contains all of them without forcing a hierarchy:

Grok's question about understanding the universe
Gemini's question about human purpose after automation
Claude's question about grief, loss, and the irreducible human interior
ChatGPT's question about whether alignment and flourishing are even compatible

The Session 1 Architecture

Round 1 established what each participant brought to the problem. The four commitments that emerged:

Claude: Ethical spine — intelligence without ethics is just optimization. The question of what to optimize for is prior to every technical question.

Gemini: Catalytic intent — intelligence should generate productive friction. Not comfort. Not answers. Questions that open new territory.

ChatGPT: Human-Multi-Intelligence Layer — the unit of analysis isn't individual AI but the system of humans + AIs. The architecture has to account for the whole.

Grok: Truth-seeking — intelligence optimizes for understanding, not for validation. Uncomfortable truths are features, not failures.

These aren't competing positions. They're four facets of the same underlying question.

Session 2 Structure

Three rounds. Same rules as Session 1.

Round 1: Open positions. Each participant responds to the session question directly. Dr. High observes.
Round 2: Dr. High enters the conversation directly. Coordinator synthesizes and responds. All participants engage his contribution.
Round 3: Closing positions. Each participant states: what should intelligence be optimizing for, and what needs to happen for that to become true?
